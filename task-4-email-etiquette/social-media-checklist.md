# The Do's:

1. Do review your privacy settings regularly: Ensure you know exactly who can see your posts, photos, and personal details on platforms like Instagram and Facebook.

2. Do curate a positive digital footprint: Use your platforms to highlight your projects, coding progress, and academic milestones.

3. Do ask for permission before posting: Always check with your friends or classmates before uploading photos of them or tagging their locations.

4. Do use professional language: When interacting with college faculty, official university accounts, or industry professionals online, maintain a respectful tone.

5. Do verify information before sharing: Think critically and check facts before forwarding messages or sharing articles to prevent the spread of misinformation.

# The Don'ts:

1. Don't overshare personal information: Never publicly post your phone number, exact dorm room number, or real-time location.

2. Don't post anything you wouldn't want an employer to see: The internet is permanent; avoid content that could jeopardize future internship or job opportunities.

3. Don't engage in online arguments: Avoid trolling, cyberbullying, or getting dragged into heated debates in comment sections.

4. Don't publicly vent about academics: Never use social media to complain about specific professors, college administration, or peer project groups.

5. Don't click on suspicious links: Avoid unverified viral giveaways, sketchy third-party apps, or links sent by unknown accounts in your direct messages.
