# The "Stay Safe Online" Checklist

# General Online Safety:

1. Verify Sender Details: Before clicking links in "urgent" emails regarding college fees or portal updates, always double-check the actual sender email address, not just the display name.

2. Beware of Open Wi-Fi: Avoid accessing banking apps or typing sensitive passwords while connected to open, public campus Wi-Fi networks.

3. Use Password Managers: Stop reusing the same password for your VITyarthi portal, GitHub, and social media. Use a password manager to generate and store complex, unique passwords.

4. Limit Digital Footprints: Regularly audit your social media privacy settings and never publicly share your exact dorm location, phone number, or daily schedule.

5. Update Software Immediately: Don't ignore system update notifications on your laptop or phone; these patches often fix critical security vulnerabilities.

6. Enable 2FA Everywhere: Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for your primary college email and GitHub accounts to block unauthorized access even if your password leaks.

# UPI & Financial Safety:

7. UPI PIN Rule: Remember that you never need to scan a QR code or enter your UPI PIN to receive money. You only enter your PIN to send money or check your balance.

8. Verify Before Sending: Always pause to verify the registered name that appears on your screen before finalizing any UPI payment, especially when sending money to unfamiliar numbers or online sellers.

# Mandatory Reporting Channels:

• National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in 

• Cyber Crime Helpline: Dial 1930 (Available 24x7)
